# Week 3 → Week 4 Pre-Reading

Week 4 is about designing your own anti-racism game. Before then, spend some time thinking about what makes educational games actually work.

## Watch

- [Extra Credits — Gamification](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=h6rDxCVmoCQ) (short, looks at when gamification works and when it's fake)
- [Raph Koster — A Theory of Fun for Game Design](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FpN9NnctQ9E) (classic talk on why games feel good to play)

## Read

- [Nielsen Norman — Gamification of online learning](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/gamification-learning/)
- [Beat Modules platform tour](https://www.beatmodules.com) (spend 10 minutes playing around — see what Oliver showed you in Week 1, but this time pay attention to the game mechanics, not the content)

## Think about

When you walk into Week 4 you'll be asked to pitch a game idea. It'll save you time if you've already started thinking about:

- What specific thing about anti-racism does my game teach? (be narrow — "recognising microaggressions" beats "all of racism")
- What does the player *do* moment-to-moment? (answer questions? make choices? sort things?)
- How does the game give feedback so the player actually learns, not just wins?

No need to pick a final idea yet — just warm up the thinking.
